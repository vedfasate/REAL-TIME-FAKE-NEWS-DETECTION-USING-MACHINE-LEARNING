How to check news authenticity

This page explains practical steps to verify whether a piece of news is reliable. Use these as a checklist when you see something suspicious.

  1. Check the source: Is the publishing domain known and reputable? Look for explicit author names and contact information.
  2. Cross-check: Search for the same story on multiple reputable outlets. If only one obscure site reports it, be skeptical.
  3. Examine dates and context: Outdated or context-less screenshots are often recycled to mislead.
  4. Look for original reporting: Reliable stories often cite primary sources such as official statements, research papers, or direct interviews.
  5. Reverse-image search: For images, use reverse-image tools to see where the picture originally appeared.
  6. Read beyond the headline: Sensational headlines may not match the story; open the article and read the full text.
